Fun Facts

July 22, 2016 by Devesh

Tweet
  1. Travel Maldives.
    2.

    Climate change and rising sea levels are of great concern to the Maldives, which is only 8 feet above sea level at its highest point. As global warming causes the polar ice caps melt and sea levels to rise, the Maldives' entire existence is in jeopardy. The government has also purchased land in other countries in order to evacuate residents in tough times. So do try to visit this beautiful country before it disappears beneath the waves.

    A post shared by Maldives Islands (@maldives.islands) on Jan 12, 2017 at 7:19am PST

  2. France is the most visited country in the world.
    3.

    It also covers the most time zones. France uses 12 different time zones, more than any other country in the world.

  3. The world’s busiest airport is Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport.
    4.

    One might expect the world’s busiest airport to be located in New York or London or LA but the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta Airport is the busiest airport in the world and is busiest since 1998. It serves around 225 destinations in more than 50 countries. There are 2,500 arrivals and departures per day bringing in more than 250,000 passengers to the airport everyday. The two biggest factors of this traffic are Delta Airlines which has more 1000 arrivals/departures everyday. The other reason is that Atlanta lies within 2 - 3 hours of almost all the US cities which makes it a popular stopover place.

  4. India is the most diverese country in the world.
    5.

    India is the most diverese countries in the world in almost every aspect. They have a super luxury royal train letting people discover their rich cultural heritage from the Taj Mahal to the forts in Rajasthan to the beautiful capital city of New Delhi.

    A post shared by LUXURY INDIANS (@luxuryindians) on Feb 27, 2017 at 4:21am PST

  5. Travel is good for health and stress.
    6.

    Studies show that travel lowers stress levels and helps productivity at work. After only a day or two, almost 90% of people experience significant drops in stress. Travel also leads to higher educational achievement. Travel has been linked to a lower risk of heart disease. A nine-year study of 12,000 men found that those who took at least one vacation per year were 30% less likely to die from heart disease. Another study showed individuals who didn’t take a vacation were twice as likely to have a heart attack.

  6. Travel leads to better relationships.
    7.

    Couples are strongly and positively affected by trips taken together. More than 75 percent of adults credit travel with improving their interpersonal relationships. Studies show family travel can also significantly reduce behavioral problems and illness in children.

  7. Finland is the most-travelling country in the world.
    8.

    Finland is the most well-travelled country in the world, with the average Finn making 7.5 trips a year, including stays at home and abroad. US comes second.

  8. Canada has the most number of lakes in the world.
    9.

    With over 3 million lakes 9% of Canadian territory is actually fresh water and over 60% of all the lakes in the world are found within its borders.

    A post shared by RAD / MANGO (@radmango) on Feb 17, 2017 at 7:40pm PST

  9. Saudi Arabia is the only country with no rivers.
    10.

    Most of Saudi Arabia's drinking water comes from desalinization plants or underground reservoirs.

  10. Australia has more kangaroos than people in the country.
    11.

    The population of Australia is 23.13 million compared to 60 million of the kangaroos in the country.
