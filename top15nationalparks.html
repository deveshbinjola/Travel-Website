  1. Yosemite National Park

    2. Yosemite is one of the best places in California. Located in the Sierra Nevada Mountains of central eastern California, the Yosemite National Park is well-loved for its stunning granite cliffs, Giant Sequoia groves, beautiful waterfalls and diverse animal and plant life.by any means from the tall Yosemite falls to the amazing Vernal and Nevada falls, from the El-Capitan to the grand Half Dome, from the beautiful rivers to the breathtaking Glacier Point. Yosemite falls itself is the fifth highest waterfall in the world and from top to bottom it is 2,425 feet. Visitors can hike among the 800 miles of trails, or drive along scenic routes to experience the beautiful landscapes and glimpse wildlife. Yosemite is really paradise to people who love nature wants to closer to it. It's a dream place for hikers.

    Best time to Visit: Anywhere between Late March and Early November is a great time for Hikes and other stuff. It starts snowing in early November so trails are closed. Road to the Glacier point is open from mis- May till September.

    Things to do: Upper/Lower Yosemite Falls, Half Dome, Glacier Point, Mirror Lake, El Capitan, Vernal and Nevada Falls.

    Time to Spend: Generally I would recommend spending minimum 2-3 days if you want to hike. Camp booking fill out way too soon so plan ahead.

  3. Rocky Mountain National Park

    4. Redwoods National Park contains some of the most scenic hiking trails in the entire world and a lush carpet of lush green vegetation towered over by the tallest trees on Earth. While most people associate it with the forest, the park also contains 40 miles of beautiful coastline supporting a diverse collection of wildlife. It has a lot of campsites in the park, one near to the coast as well.

    Best time to Visit: Anywhere between Late March and October is a great time for Hikes and other stuff. It starts snowing in early November so trails are closed. The scenic drive closes in early October. I went there for thanksgiving weekend and we still hiked with spikes, so its also an option. But colorado in Spring and Fall is surreal.

    Things to do: Scenic drive, Lakes.

    Time to Spend: Generally I would recommend spending minimum 2 days if you want to hike. Camp booking fill out way too soon so plan ahead. There are a lot of hotels nearby though.

  4. Sequioa National Parl

    5. Redwoods are taller, but Sequoia is home to the largest trees in the world, by volume. The General Sherman, which is the biggest tree in the world, it's trunk has a volume of 1,487 cubic metres and is estimated to weigh over 2,000 tonnes. The underground Crystal Cave features streams and striking rock formations. Moro Rock is a granite dome offering sweeping park views. Nearby is the Tunnel Tree, a toppled tree cut to accommodate the road.

    Best time to Visit: Anywhere between Late March and Early November is a great time for Hikes and other stuff. It starts snowing in early November so trails are closed. Road to the Glacier point is open from mis- May till September.

  5. Denali Ntional Park

    6. Although regarded as the hottest place in North America, Death Valley offers a variety of historic sites and nature viewing. From snow-capped mountains to colorful sand dunes, the unique desert landscapes of Death Valley are like no other place in California. Places of interests include the scenic vista of Zabriskie Point and historic sites like Scotty’s Castle, the Wildrose Charcoal Kilns and ruins of ghost towns. This harsh climate creates an amazing opportunity to witness barren wide open lands as well as natural wonders such as the "Racetrack Playa" where stones seem to be moving by themselves across the desert floor. You can also see Joshua trees there.

    Best time to Visit: Anywhere between Late March and Early November is a great time for Hikes and other stuff. It starts snowing in early November so trails are closed. Road to the Glacier point is open from mis- May till September.

    9. San Francisco is a beautiful city in northern California, famous for its attractions and tech companies. Undoubtedly, the Golden Gate Bridge is the city’s number one attraction. Visitors can also visit Lombard Street, which is famous for its tight curves, or take a trip to Chinatown, which is the largest outside of Asia. And then, of course, there are the other big tourist attractions — Fisherman’s Wharf, Pier 39, Twin Peaks and the Alcatraz Island.
