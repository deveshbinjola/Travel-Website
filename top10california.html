Top 11 Places to visit in California.

California is often quoted as "The America of America". It boasts everything from the hottest place in the world (Death valley) to Lake Tahoe which sometimes goes to -20 degrees, from the tall Redwood forests to large Sequioa trees in Sequioa National Park, from the ever beautiful Yoesimte Valley which is around 4000 years old to the Silicon Valley where everyday a new thing is invented which can change the world in its own ways. Welcome to the Dream, Welcome to California.

  1. Yosemite National Park

    2. Yosemite is one of the best places in California. Located in the Sierra Nevada Mountains of central eastern California, the Yosemite National Park is well-loved for its stunning granite cliffs, Giant Sequoia groves, beautiful waterfalls and diverse animal and plant life.by any means from the tall Yosemite falls to the amazing Vernal and Nevada falls, from the El-Capitan to the grand Half Dome, from the beautiful rivers to the breathtaking Glacier Point. Yosemite falls itself is the fifth highest waterfall in the world and from top to bottom it is 2,425 feet. Visitors can hike among the 800 miles of trails, or drive along scenic routes to experience the beautiful landscapes and glimpse wildlife. Yosemite is really paradise to people who love nature wants to closer to it. It's a dream place for hikers.

  2. Pacific Coast Highway 1

    3. A beautiful driving experience, on the 123-mile route along the central California coast takes about five hours to complete at a leisurely pace. Designated as the nation's most scenic drive has a lot of beautiful stops in the way like Big Sur, McWay Falls, Bixby bridge and many other amazing views on the way. The best way to enjoy the drive is to go on a bright and sunny day from Monterey to Morro Bay.

  3. Redwood National Forest / Fern Canyon

    4. Redwoods National Park contains some of the most scenic hiking trails in the entire world and a lush carpet of lush green vegetation towered over by the tallest trees on Earth. While most people associate it with the forest, the park also contains 40 miles of beautiful coastline supporting a diverse collection of wildlife. It has a lot of campsites in the park, one near to the coast as well.

  4. Sequioa National Forest

    5. Redwoods are taller, but Sequoia is home to the largest trees in the world, by volume. The General Sherman, which is the biggest tree in the world, it's trunk has a volume of 1,487 cubic metres and is estimated to weigh over 2,000 tonnes. The underground Crystal Cave features streams and striking rock formations. Moro Rock is a granite dome offering sweeping park views. Nearby is the Tunnel Tree, a toppled tree cut to accommodate the road.
