The Four States

Spring Break trip across California, Nevada, Utah and Arizona

Road trip.

July 22, 2016 by Devesh

Our trip map.

Day 1 Thursday: (Leave Sacramento early to Las Vegas, Nevada via Death Valley)
We have to reach Las Vegas by 6:00 PM. The journey via Death Valley is around 10 hours. We have around 2-3 hours for stops and other stuff to do in Death Valley.

A photo posted by The Travel Student (@deveshbinjola) on Oct 30, 2016 at 12:42am PDT


After reaching Vegas: Dinner with old friend.(His Treat) Then see saw the Strip and other hotels like(Paris, Venetian, Bellagio (watched the fountains), Stratosphere) and Casino's and Club all night. We can take our time here and have fun.
Death Valley National Park (California).

HOTEL in Las Vegas: DoubleTree By Hilton (20,000 Hilton Points) + 4 Breakfast coupons as a Daimond Member.
Regular Cost of Hotel = $170 + $120 ($30 each for a Breakfast Coupon) = $290.
I rate 1 Hilton Point as 0.5 cent, so a great deal.

Fountains at Bellagio, backdrop of Paris Hotel, Las Vegas.

Day 2, Friday: As we have partied late last night, we needed some rest so we can wake up till 11:00 AM and leave hotel by 12:00 PM (Late Checkout for HHnonors Daimond). Though we woke up around 8:00 AM, had our Breakfast an again slept till 11:00 AM. Went to a casino again to waste some money. Left for Zion National Park around 2:00 PM after having lunch. We reached Zion by 4:30 PM via our Hotel at Hurricane, Utah. We also checked with information desk about the places we can see there. We reahed late but still saw something today and did a bus trip of the park and spotted the few good places for next day hikes and also saw the sunset. We also did a small hike for around 30 minutes round trip and returned back to the parking by the last bus which was around 8:30 PM. Then we headed back to our hotel which was 20 - 30 min away from Zion National Park (The cheapest, closest and best place I could find).

Welcome to Zion National Park.

Hotel in Zion : Rodeway INN ( 97$ after 25$OFF (got a deal))

Day 3, Saturday (Day fully dedicated to Zion): Our motto was to rest properly last night and leave early for the park for a full day adventure. We had our breakfast at the Hotel (was OK as compared to Hilton). We also had some food to go for our lunch.
We reached the park around 8:00 and it took us half an hour to reach our stop for the first hike. We started with Angel's Landing. By far the hardest hikes I have done. Would rate it 2nd in USA after Half Dome. It was a tough hike; really tough for last half mile. But the view from the top was so awesome that the hike was worth it. The hike was very strenous. We were back at the bus stop by 1:00 PM.

A photo posted by The Travel Student (@deveshbinjola) on Sep 23, 2016 at 5:03pm PDT

So we had our lunch after the hike. Our next hike was Lower Emerald Pool. It was a 1 hour round trip. The hike was easy.
Our third and the last hike for the day was The Narrows (Walking by the River). We did not finish the hike as it was a really long hike. So we left for Page, Arizona around 8:00 PM. We had our dinner at Pizza Hut somewhere around the border of Utah. We reached Page, Arizona around 11:30 Pm. It was a really bad drive especialy around first 30 miles from Zion. I try to drive during evenings because it gives me the day to watch the beautiful things around me. We stayed in our hotel in Page, AZ so that we don't waste time in traveling next morning. The drive from Zion to Page was around 1.5 - 2 hours but it took us more because of te dinner.

Good Places to see in Zion: Emerald Pools, Weeping Rock, Angel's Landing (Best), Canyon Overlook, The Narrows and Riverside walk and more.

Hotel in Page: Quality Inn At Lake Powell (97$)

Day 4, Sunday (Day fully dedicated to Page): Page is a really beautiful place and we had our hotel in really good and beautiful location, as we have a really great view from our window. It is a 7 min drive from Horse Shoe Bend, 15 min from Antelope Canyon, 5 min from Lake Powell and Glen Canyon Dam. We had our breakfast at Hotel. Breakfast was Good; somewhere between Hilton and Rodeway Inn.
Horse Shoe Bend, Page (Arizona).

So we started with Horse Shoe bend and we spent like 2 hours exploring it and taking pictures. Then after fully exploring the beautiful Horse Shoe bend we headed to Lake Powell and Glen Canyon Dam. It is a good place to see and if you have enough time, you can go a boat ride or dam tour or any other tour offered there. We stayed there around 1.5 hours and then went to Lower Antelope Canyons.
Lower Antelope Canyons, Page (Arizona).

Lower Antelope Canyons was the best spot to see in Page. I will rate it as one of the top spots in US. So we had to takd a guided tour to see the Lower Canyons. The price for guided tour was $33 per person. It was worth it. I would recommend to go for the first tour in the morning as it is the best time to see the canyons. Be sure to ask your guide to update our camera settings for better pictures. Try to stay with your guide as he will show you some awesome spots and take your best pictures. All the guides are really nice and love their jobs. The place is managed by Native Americans so they take pride in showing up their heritage. The guides are so nice that you will be more than happy to tip them. The tour was around 1.5 hours. WE really loved the tour. We finished our tour by 3:30 PM and then headed towards Grand Canyon.
Lower Antelope Canyons, Page (Arizona).

We reached Grand Canyon by 6:00 PM and saw 2-3 spots and also saw the sunset. Then we headed to our hotel around 7:30 PM which was in Flagstaff, Arizon around 1.5 hours drive from the Grand Canyon National Park. I did not realize that I am booking the hotel so far away from Grand Canyon. We reached Flagstaff by 9:00 PM, had dinner at really good Indian restaurant. Then we headed to our hotel and had a good sleep.
Glen Canyon Dam, Page (Arizona).

Hotel in Flagstaff: Baymont Inn and Suites ($61).

Day 5, Monday: Frankly we had to leave today back to Sacramento just because of me, because I had my presentation Tuesday morning at 9:00 AM ( nothing else) so it was be fine for us if we reached late and my other three friends had nothing next day. Our plan for Monday was to start early for Grand Canyon and see few good spots within 6 hours we have and then head back home. There are three bus routes in Grand Canyon. We had a 2 mile hike which was Billion years hike overlooking the beautiful canyons around the even gorgeous Colorado River.
We then headed to the Red Line Bus (the bus with the most beautiful stops). We stopped at around 5 points which Hopi Point, Pima Point, and the last stop on red line: Hermits Rest.
Hermits Rest, Grand Canyon National Park(Arizona): last stop on red line bus.

We then had our lunch in a closeby Subway and Carl's Junior and then headed back for Sacramento at around 3:00 PM. The drive from Grand Canyon to Sacramento with rest/pit stops was around 13-14 hours. This was the worst point of our trip as we had to leave all the fun to go back to school and drive so much to reach back there on time. We had three pit stops while coming back including one for dinner at McDonalds. We reached Sacramento around 4:30 AM. I slept for few hours and then went to school for my Project presentation and as you would have thought; I completely nailed it.

This trip was amazing and by far the best Spring trip I ever had. I hope we had more time to explore things more but as students we had our schedules really tight. This was my last spring break as I will be graduating this December but I hope I will make a much more happening trip next year. We have planned one which is of around 8-10 days by taking a break from work which includes the East Coast. Lets see how it plans out to be.

Few Tips:

  • All the hotels I booked were refundable, so no worries but if in case we wanted to cancel any hotel.
  • Breakfast is free in every hotel and you should compulsory try to have it. It saves a lot of your money.
  • Personally I feel driving at night or late evening saves time if you have a tight schedule.
