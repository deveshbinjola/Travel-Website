Credit Card Churn

Making 300000+ miles in 15 minutes.

December 23, 2017 by The Travel Student

Tweet

So, as my credit card applications in last 24 months went under 5, I thought I should capitalize on that and I started to apply for the credit cards I need and rack up some points. So I applied for 4 credit cards and racked up 300,000 miles and points in a single day basically within 15 minutesand without even getting a shot at my credit score.

Which cards did I apply for?

  • Chase Ink Preferred
  • Chase United Mileage Plus
  • CITI / AADVANTAGE PLATINUM SELECT WORLD ELITE MASTERCARD
  • AAdvantage Aviator Red World Elite Mastercard from Barclays
  • Citi / AAdvantage Gold World Elite Mastercard

I am appling for Citi / AAdvantage Gold World Elite Mastercard after 8 days of applying Citi AADVANTAGE Gold World Elite Mastercard because of Citi's 8 day rule of applying for cards. You cannot apply more than another Citi card within 8 days of applying a card. Check this page for all the rules about credit card applications.

I would seriously like to warn and advice to all the readers not to do this until you have a good idea about your credit score and you already have sufficient credit cards and history in order to take the toll. I did it because it was a time which came for me after 2 years and I already have all the Chase branded cards. I am also planning for a few internal trips so these points will do help me for 2-3 business class round trips. So doing this churn only cost me $285, $145 after 1st year if I really want to keep these cards after that. So if you have a similar situation now or in the future then only try this.
